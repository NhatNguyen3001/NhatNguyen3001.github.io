IT works

IT support technician

For an IT support technician, you have to know everything a little bit from sofware development to fxing bugs. They usually interact with customers, with clients through phone calls or meet in person and also co-workers as well. They can work in the company or stay at home if their schedule is set to work at home that day. The challenging aspects when working as an IT support technician is that you have to deal with complex problems and you might don’t have the tools or the technologies to fix it.

Cyber security specialist

As an Cyber Security Specialist, you usually check logs or abnormalities, which is recorded or reported by a member in the comapany or from your team. They usually have meetings and work in a group of people. They work in their office, sometimes they go to the mainserver room to check for errors or fixing problems. The challenging aspects when working as a Cyber Security Specialist is that they have to be careful when checking abnormalities and have to set up protection to protect data from attacks outside.

Software Engineer

A day of a software engineer starts with many tasks. They usually have a Github account, where they can push their code, join another person’s project or invite someone to do their projects. As an software engineer, you have to able to do many tasks from enabling features to fixing bugs in the program, writng scripts. They also have meetings, internal trainings, pair programming. They usually work in a team and their supervisor. There are a lot of meetings, hangouts. Usually they work in their office but they can also work from home as well. Working as a software engineer, you have to be able to cope with many problems. You have to adapt to the latest technologies and customer’s demands. Sometimes you will have to work under time pressure with limited resources or infrastructures. And finally, you might have conflicts with the testing team.

Game programmer

Game programmers usually start their day pretty late when compared to other “white-collar” jobs. They check mails and then start to write and debugging code. They usually have meetings to keep track of the progress. When a project is almost complete, sometimes they have to work overtime. They work in office. The challenging aspects is that sometimes, they have to work overtime too much, which can be detrimental to their health and their personal’s life.

IT manager

Being an IT manager also means that you have to get involed in whatever your employees do. You will write reports, fix problems and write querries. You will be working with project managers, managers from other departments. You will also working directly with customers, clients that you responsible for. Most of the time you will be working in the company. However, sometimes, you will have to travel aboard to work. As an IT manager, you have to face many challenges. The latest technologies will not always suit the current company that you are working in. Cybersecurity and data management is the main problems of IT managers today.
